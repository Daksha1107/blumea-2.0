## Creating a New Article

### Step 1: Start a Draft

1. Click "New Article" in the admin dashboard
2. Choose a topic category (Serums, Moisturizers, etc.)
3. Select or create an author profile

### Step 2: Write Your Content

#### Title Guidelines
- **Length**: 50-60 characters
- **Format**: Include primary keyword
- **Style**: Clear, descriptive, benefit-focused
- **Examples**:
  - ‚úÖ "Best Vitamin C Serums for Hyperpigmentation in 2025"
  - ‚úÖ "How to Choose the Right Moisturizer for Oily Skin"
  - ‚ùå "Amazing Products" (too vague)
  - ‚ùå "This is the Best Serum You'll Ever Use in Your Entire Life" (too long)

#### Summary/Excerpt
- **Length**: 150-160 characters
- **Purpose**: Appears in search results and social shares
- **Content**: Hook that makes readers want to learn more
- **Example**: "Discover the science-backed vitamin C serums dermatologists recommend for fading dark spots and brightening skin. Compare ingredients, prices, and results."

#### Article Body

Use this structure for optimal readability:

```markdown
## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Hook: Relatable problem or question
- Promise: What readers will learn
- Credibility: Why trust this article

## What You Need to Know
- Key facts or background information
- Scientific context (cite sources)

## Main Content Sections
### Subtopic 1
- Detailed explanation
- Practical tips
- Examples

### Subtopic 2
- Continue with logical flow
- Use bullet points for lists
- Include internal links

## Product Reviews (if applicable)
### Product 1 Name
- Key ingredients
- Benefits
- Best for (skin type/concern)
- Price range
- Where to buy

## How to Use / Application Tips
- Step-by-step instructions
- Frequency recommendations
- What to combine/avoid

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- List 3-5 common errors
- Explain why they're problematic
- Provide correct alternatives

## Final Thoughts
- Summarize key takeaways
- Call to action
- Encourage comments/questions
```

### Step 3: Optimize for SEO

#### Target Keyword
- Choose one primary keyword
- Use it in: title, first paragraph, one H2, meta description
- Don't over-optimize (keyword stuffing)

#### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Compelling call to action
- Example: "Learn which vitamin C serum works best for your skin type. Compare top-rated products backed by dermatologists. Get clearer skin in 8 weeks."

#### Keywords
Add 5-10 relevant keywords:
- Primary keyword
- Secondary keywords (synonyms, related terms)
- Long-tail variations
- Example: "vitamin c serum", "ascorbic acid", "skin brightening", "hyperpigmentation treatment"

#### Internal Links
- Link to 3-5 related articles
- Use descriptive anchor text
- Example: "Learn more about [choosing the right cleanser](#)"

#### External Links
- Link to 2-3 authoritative sources
- Cite scientific studies when making claims
- Link to product manufacturers (if reviewing)

## E-E-A-T Guidelines

### Experience (First E)
Show real-world experience:
- "After testing 15 vitamin C serums over 3 months..."
- "In my 10 years as a skincare formulator..."
- Include before/after results (with disclaimer)

### Expertise
Demonstrate knowledge:
- Explain the science (but keep it accessible)
- Use correct terminology
- Reference studies: "According to a 2023 study in the Journal of Dermatology..."

### Authoritativeness
Build credibility:
- Author credentials (displayed automatically)
- Link to author profile with full bio
- Cite expert opinions: "Dr. Jane Smith, board-certified dermatologist, recommends..."

### Trustworthiness
Be honest and transparent:
- Disclose affiliate relationships
- Mention product drawbacks, not just benefits
- Update articles when new information emerges
- Add "Last updated: [date]" for evergreen content

## Content Structure

### Headings Hierarchy

```
# Article Title (H1) - Automatically rendered
## Main Sections (H2) - Use for primary topics
### Subsections (H3) - Use for subtopics
#### Minor Points (H4) - Use sparingly
```

### Formatting Tips

- **Bold**: For emphasis and important terms
- *Italic*: For subtle emphasis
- Lists: For easy scanning
- Tables: For comparing products
- Blockquotes: For expert quotes

### Reading Level
- Write for 8th-9th grade reading level
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences)
- Simple sentence structure
- Define technical terms

## Images and Media

### Featured Image
- Required for every article
- Dimensions: 1200x630px (optimal for social sharing)
- Alt text: Descriptive, includes keyword
- Example: "Woman applying vitamin C serum to face"

### In-Article Images
- Use every 300-400 words
- Before/after photos (with consent)
- Product shots
- Infographics explaining concepts
- Always add descriptive alt text

### Alt Text Guidelines
- Describe what's in the image
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Keep under 125 characters
- Don't say "image of" or "picture of"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes
1. **Keyword Stuffing**: Overusing keywords unnaturally
2. **Thin Content**: Articles under 800 words
3. **No Sources**: Making claims without citations
4. **Promotional Tone**: Sounding like an advertisement
5. **Outdated Info**: Not updating old articles

### SEO Mistakes
1. **Duplicate Titles**: Using same title as another article
2. **Missing Meta Description**: Letting search engines generate it
3. **Broken Links**: Links to removed or moved pages
4. **No Alt Text**: Missing image descriptions
5. **Poor URL Structure**: Using auto-generated slugs

### Formatting Mistakes
1. **Wall of Text**: No paragraph breaks
2. **Missing Headings**: Not breaking up content
3. **Inconsistent Style**: Mixing formats randomly
4. **No Lists**: When bullet points would be clearer
5. **Too Many Fonts/Colors**: Keep it simple
